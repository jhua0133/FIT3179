Visitors of Australia

Exploring where visitors are coming from and where they are going

Where are Visitors going in Australia?

Visitors per 1000 people in each state (2022)

Explore the distribution of visitors across Australian states using this Choropleth Map, which visualizes the number of visitors per 1,000 residents. Darker shades represent higher visitor density, indicating regions with a greater influx of tourists. Lighter shades correspond to areas with lower visitor density. The map provides an immediate comparison of tourism intensity by state. Use the interactive features to hover over each state for detailed data on visitors. This visualization helps uncover patterns in tourism, offering insights how popular a state is in relation to its population size.

Comparing past and recent visitor numbers

Compare historical and recent visitor numbers to Australian states with this Stacked Bar Chart. The chart displays total visitors in millions across different states, comparing two time periods: 2008-09 and 2018-19. Each bar is divided to show the contribution of visitors during both periods, making it easy to see changes in tourism over time. This visualization helps highlight states that have experienced significant growth or decline in visitor numbers. Hover over the bars for detailed figures, and observe how tourism trends differ across Australia's regions.

Where are visitors coming from?

Visitors by Top 5 Countries Over Time

This line chart presents the number of visitors from the top five countries to Australia over time, from 2009 to 2019. The countries represented include China, New Zealand, USA, UK, and Japan. Each line illustrates the trend of visitors from these countries, highlighting changes in tourism patterns over the decade. The pink annotation emphasizes China's significant growth, with a 368% increase in visitors. Use the dropdown below the chart to filter the data and view the trend for individual countries or all countries together. Hover over the lines for specific data points per year.

Visitors Metrics for Top 5 Countries

This radar chart compares visitor metrics across five countries: China, New Zealand, USA, UK, and Japan. The chart displays various categories such as "Visitors for Business," "Visitors for Leisure," "Short Term Visitors," "Long Term Visitors," and "Repeat Visitors." Each axis represents one of these categories, and the points on the chart reflect the percentage of visitors in each category from each country. The closer a data point is to the outer edge, the higher the percentage of visitors in that category for that country. This visualization allows for easy comparison of tourism trends across different countries, helping to identify which countries have a higher proportion of business or leisure travelers, repeat visitors, or long-term versus short-term stays.
